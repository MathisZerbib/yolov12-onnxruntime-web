## Design Context

### Users

Players follow live public traffic cameras, understand an active prediction round at a glance, choose an outcome, and place a wallet-backed position with confidence. Operators configure rooms, zones, contracts, and role wallets without needing to understand the underlying deployment machinery.

### Brand Personality

Charged, precise, and trustworthy. The player surface should feel like a premium live-event trading floor with restrained gaming energy. The backoffice should feel calm, guarded, and operational.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use the existing dark live-video HUD and acid-lime signal color as a recognizable broadcast language. Reward moments can glow and move, but transactional states must remain sober and explicit. Admin surfaces use the same identity in a brighter, quieter control-plane treatment. Avoid generic casino clichés, decorative neon overload, glass-card repetition, and motion that competes with detection.

### Design Principles

1. Make the active round, remaining time, selected outcome, stake, and transaction state understandable in one glance.
2. Keep real-time inference work outside React's high-frequency render path; animation must use transforms and opacity.
3. Use progressive disclosure in admin workflows, with one obvious next action and actionable recovery copy.
4. Preserve every core action on mobile with 44px touch targets and a thumb-reachable betting action area.
5. Treat trust as part of the interface: clearly distinguish live, stale, waiting, unavailable, and confirmed states.
